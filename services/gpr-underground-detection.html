GPR Underground Detection in Gauteng & South Africa

Know what's below before you dig. Ground-penetrating radar locates buried utilities, voids, and anomalies up to 5m deep – without breaking a single surface.

What is Ground-Penetrating Radar?

Ground-Penetrating Radar (GPR) transmits high-frequency electromagnetic pulses into the ground and records the reflected signals from sub-surface objects and layer interfaces. By analysing travel times and signal strength, a trained GPR operator can identify, locate, and map buried features without any excavation.

For project managers and engineers, GPR means you can confidently plan excavations knowing the exact position of buried services – eliminating the risk of costly strikes, project shutdowns, utility damage, and the personal danger to workers from hitting live electrical cables or gas mains.

What GPR Can Detect

Electrical Cables & Conduits

High and low voltage cables, fibre optic conduits, and telecommunications infrastructure.

Water & Sewer Mains

Water supply pipes, stormwater culverts, and sewer reticulation – both metallic and non-metallic.

Gas Pipelines

Natural gas and LPG distribution pipelines including plastic and steel variants.

Voids & Sinkholes

Sub-surface cavities, abandoned mine workings, and dolomite void mapping.

Project Case Study

CASE STUDY · TSHWANE CBD, GAUTENG

Underground Utility Mapping Before CBD Excavation

A civil contractor was about to excavate a 3-metre-deep trench in a congested CBD block for new stormwater infrastructure. Municipal as-built records were incomplete and over 30 years old.

Mathibe's GPR team surveyed the 800m² area over two days, identifying 23 buried services absent from municipal records – including two 11kV power cables and a high-pressure water main. The updated utility plan was integrated into the contractor's BIM model before any excavation began.

Result: Zero utility strikes. Zero project delays. Estimated savings: R2.3M in damage liability.
